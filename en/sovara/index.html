How Sovara works.

What happens when an app accesses your microphone?

Four moments. From access to alert.

01
Detection

An app turns on your microphone.

Maps. WhatsApp. A game. Even an app you didn't open. Android shows a tiny dot in the corner. That's all you get.

02
Sovara sees it

Sovara sees it instantly.

Sovara queries the system every moment to check microphone state. The moment access starts, it knows. Latency: under 1 second.

03
Identification

Sovara names the culprit.

Both the calling app AND the foreground app are identified. Most competitors only show what's on screen. Sovara shows who actually requested microphone access.

04
Alert

You get notified — your way.

Sound (8 options + custom). Vibration (5 patterns). Push notification. LED flash. Choose what fits your moment.

How it stays 100% local.

Three technical facts you can verify.

No internet permission

Sovara's manifest declares no INTERNET permission. Android enforces this. The app physically cannot make a network request — not even to a Sovara server (because there is no Sovara server).

No audio capture in code

We use RECORD_AUDIO to query microphone state, but the APK contains zero calls to MediaRecorder.start(), AudioRecord.read(), or any audio capture method. Anyone can decompile and verify.

No third-party SDK

Zero Firebase, zero Crashlytics, zero Analytics, zero ad SDK. Build configuration is open: you can audit the dependency tree.
