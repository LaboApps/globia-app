Quick pitch

One-liner (≈10 words)

Sovara is an Android privacy guard that alerts you in real time when an app accesses your microphone or camera.

Short paragraph (≈50 words)

Sovara is an Android application by indie studio LocalFirst Apps that monitors microphone and camera access in real time. Unlike the native Android indicator, Sovara identifies the calling app, alerts via sound, vibration and notification, keeps an exportable history, and operates 100% locally with zero third-party trackers.

Full paragraph (≈130 words)

Android's microphone and camera indicator — the green dot in the corner — tells you something is happening, but not who or why. Sovara fills that gap. Developed by indie studio LocalFirst Apps, Sovara runs silently in the background and fires a multimodal alert — sound, vibration, push notification, or LED flash — the instant any app accesses your microphone or camera. It names the app. It logs the event. It lets you whitelist trusted apps so you only hear about the ones that matter.

Unlike competing apps, Sovara requests no INTERNET permission, includes no third-party SDKs, and collects zero data. The claim is verifiable: decompile the APK. Sovara is available on Android 12+ at a one-time price of $4.99 USD, with no subscription. Public launch: May–June 2026.

About LocalFirst Apps

LocalFirst Apps is an independent mobile studio built around one principle: your data belongs on your device. We build tools that work entirely offline — no servers, no accounts, no hidden data flows. Every permission we request, we can justify. Every claim we make, you can verify.

Sovara is our first product. Others will follow, all built on the same foundation. We believe privacy should be a default, not a premium feature, and that the burden of proof should rest with the developer — not the user.
